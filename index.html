Moroccan Dama Rules

Board & Pieces

  • Board Setup: Played on an 8x8 checkerboard oriented so that the bottom-left square is dark.
  • Playable Squares: All movement occurs exclusively on the light (white) squares.
  • Starting Material: Each player starts with 12 pieces arranged on their first three ranks.

Movement

  • Men (Standard Pieces): Move diagonally forward only, one square at a time. They cannot move or capture backwards.
  • Kings (Dama): When a man reaches the opponent's back rank, it is promoted to a King. Kings are "flying kings"—they can move any number of empty squares along a diagonal, forwards or backwards.

Capturing

  • Jumping: Capturing is performed by jumping over an opponent's piece into an empty square immediately behind it.
  • Men Captures: Men capture diagonally forward only. They cannot jump backwards.
  • King Captures: Kings capture by jumping over an opponent's piece along a diagonal from a distance, and can land on any empty square behind that captured piece.
  • Multi-Jumps: If a piece can make another jump from its landing square, it must continue capturing within the same turn. Captured pieces are removed only after the turn ends.

Spanish Majority Rules (Quantity & Quality)

  • Quantity Priority: If multiple capturing paths exist, you must choose the path that captures the maximum number of pieces.
  • Quality Priority: If two paths capture the same number of pieces, you must choose the path that captures the maximum number of Kings.

Traditional Nfekh (Huffing) Rule

  • If Nfekh Mode is enabled, you can make a normal move even when a capture is available (which is normally blocked).
  • However, if you bypass a capture, the opponent's "Nfekh!" button will light up.
  • By clicking it, they can select and "blow away" (remove) your offending piece that missed the jump. The opponent then continues to play their turn as normal!
